Full Citation:
Deng, Y., Bao, F., Kong, Y., Ren, Z., & Dai, Q. (2017). Deep direct reinforcement learning for
financial signal representation and trading. IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning
Systems, 28(3), 653-664.

DOI: 10.1109/TNNLS.2016.2522401

Publisher: IEEE
Published: March 2017 (accepted/online 2016)
ISSN: 2162-237X

URL: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/7407387

Abstract:
This paper proposes a recurrent deep neural network for real-time financial signal
representation and trading. The architecture combines deep learning for automatic feature
extraction from dynamic market conditions with reinforcement learning for trading decision
making under uncertainty. The authors introduce a task-aware backpropagation through time
(TBPTT) method to address the gradient vanishing problem in deep training. The model
directly optimizes risk-adjusted returns including the differential Sharpe ratio and accounts
for transaction costs. Experiments on commodity futures (crude oil, gold, natural gas, corn)
demonstrate that the proposed approach outperforms existing techniques including random
forests and multi-layer perceptrons.

Key Contributions:
- Deep Direct Reinforcement Learning (DDRL) framework combining CNN/RNN with RL
- Task-aware BPTT for improved gradient flow
- End-to-end optimization of risk-adjusted portfolio returns
- Handles transaction costs directly in reward function

Relevance to Thesis:
Foundational paper on applying deep RL directly to trading/portfolio decisions.
Demonstrates feasibility of replacing traditional technical indicators with
end-to-end learned representations for financial signal processing.
